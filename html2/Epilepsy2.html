What is Epilepsy?

Epilepsy is a chronic neurological disorder that causes recurrent seizures due to abnormal electrical activity in the brain. The severity and frequency of seizures vary from person to person.

Common Symptoms

  • Sudden convulsive seizures or loss of consciousness.
  • Muscle spasms in the arms or legs.
  • Blank staring or momentary unresponsiveness.
  • Sudden changes in behavior or mood.
  • Confusion or fatigue after the seizure ends.

Tips and Advice

  • Take prescribed medications regularly without interruption.
  • Avoid sleep deprivation, stress, and fatigue, as they may trigger seizures.
  • Stay away from strong lights or flashing patterns if they trigger your condition.
  • Always carry a medical ID card indicating that you have epilepsy in case of emergency.
  • Practice relaxation and deep breathing exercises to reduce stress.

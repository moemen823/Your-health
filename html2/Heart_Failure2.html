What is Heart Failure?

Heart failure is a condition where the heart is unable to pump enough blood to meet the bodyâ€™s needs for oxygen and nutrients. This does not mean the heart has stopped working, but that it has become weaker and less efficient.

Common Symptoms

  • Shortness of breath during activity or while lying down.
  • Swelling in the feet or ankles.
  • Persistent fatigue or weakness.
  • Rapid weight gain due to fluid retention.
  • Chronic cough, especially at night.

Tips and Guidelines

  • Follow a low-sodium diet to reduce fluid retention.
  • Limit fluid intake if advised by your doctor.
  • Take prescribed medications regularly without interruption.
  • Monitor your weight daily and inform your doctor if you notice sudden increases.
  • Engage in light physical activity regularly according to your ability.

When to See a Doctor Immediately

  • Severe or sudden shortness of breath.
  • Rapid swelling in the legs or abdomen.
  • Fainting or severe dizziness.
  • Chest pain or irregular heartbeat.

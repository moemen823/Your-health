What Are Heart Valve Diseases?

Heart valves are gates that allow blood to flow in the correct direction inside the heart. When these valves become damaged, narrowed, or leaky, blood flow is disrupted, leading to heart strain and difficulty in performing its function.

Common Symptoms

  • Shortness of breath, especially during exertion or when lying down.
  • Fatigue and general body weakness.
  • Swelling in the feet or ankles.
  • Palpitations or irregular heartbeat.
  • Chest pain or heaviness at times.

Tips and Guidelines

  • Regularly follow up with your cardiologist to monitor valve condition.
  • Stick to prescribed medications such as blood thinners if recommended.
  • Avoid excessive physical effort unless approved by your doctor.
  • Maintain a healthy weight and reduce salt intake in your diet.
  • Inform your doctor before any surgery or dental procedure to prevent bacterial infection.

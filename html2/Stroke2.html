What is a Stroke?

A stroke is a medical emergency that occurs when the blood flow to a part of the brain is interrupted, leading to brain cell damage within minutes. It may result from a blockage in blood vessels or bleeding inside the brain.

Common Symptoms

  • Sudden weakness or numbness in the face, arm, or leg, especially on one side of the body.
  • Difficulty speaking or understanding speech.
  • Sudden confusion or loss of balance.
  • Severe sudden headache with no known cause.
  • Sudden vision problems in one or both eyes.

Prevention Tips

  • Keep your blood pressure within a normal range.
  • Exercise regularly and maintain a healthy diet.
  • Avoid smoking and alcohol consumption.
  • Monitor your blood sugar and cholesterol levels.
  • Seek medical help immediately if sudden symptoms appear.

When to Seek Medical Help Immediately

  • If any of the above symptoms appear suddenly.
  • If you experience sudden weakness or numbness on one side of the body.
  • If speech becomes slurred or hard to understand.

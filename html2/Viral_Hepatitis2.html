What is Viral Hepatitis?

Viral hepatitis is an infection that affects the liver, caused by one of the hepatitis viruses (A, B, C, D, or E). It leads to inflammation and damage to liver cells. The methods of transmission and symptoms vary depending on the type of virus.

Common Symptoms

  • Yellowing of the skin and eyes (jaundice).
  • General fatigue and persistent tiredness.
  • Nausea or loss of appetite.
  • Dark urine and pale-colored stool.
  • Pain or heaviness in the upper right side of the abdomen.

Tips and Recommendations

  • Eat well-cooked food and wash your hands before meals.
  • Avoid sharing personal items such as razors or toothbrushes.
  • Never use unsterilized needles or medical tools.
  • Follow your doctorâ€™s prescribed medications, especially for hepatitis B and C.
  • Get vaccinated for hepatitis A and B to prevent infection.

When to See a Doctor Immediately

  • If you notice yellowing of the skin or eyes.
  • If you feel severe fatigue or persistent nausea.
  • If you experience strong abdominal pain or changes in urine color.
